Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ^1H, ^2H, and ^3H. ^1H and ^2H are stable, while ^3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4]nb 1] Heuer isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosecand (10−21 s). [5][6]


IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas.
