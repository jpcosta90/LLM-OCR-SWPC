Isotopes of hydrogen
Isotopes of hydrogen: ¹H, ²H, and ³H. ¹H and ²H are naturally occurring, while ³H has a half-life of 12.32 years. (4)[nb 1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zetaception (¹₀⁻²¹ s). [5][6]
Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ¹H, and ²H. ¹H and ²H are stable, while ³H has a half-life of 12.32 years.[4][nb 1] 
Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zetaception (10⁻²¹ s). [5][6]
IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
