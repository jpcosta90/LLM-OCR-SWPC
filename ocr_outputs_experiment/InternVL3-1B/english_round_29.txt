Isotopes of hydrogen
Article
Talk

Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: \(^1H,\) and \(^{2}^{1}H,\) and \(^{3}H\) . \(^{1}H\) and \(^{2}^{1}H\) are stable, while \(^{3}H\) has a half-life of 12.32 years. \(^{1}H\) and \(^{2}^{1}H\) are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptecond (10^{-21} s). \[bold[4]oldsymbol {nb}1\)
