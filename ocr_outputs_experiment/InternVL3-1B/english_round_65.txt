Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen (¹H) has three naturally occurring isotope: \( ^1H \), and \( ^3H \). \( ^1H \) and \( ^2H \) are stable, while \( ^3H \) has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb 1] Hydro isotope also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zetocpend (10⁻² s). [5][6]

Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zetocpend (10⁻² s). [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: \( ^2H \) is deuterium [7] and \( ^3H \) is tritium [8].
