Isotopes of hydrogen

Isotopes: \( ^1H, ^2H, and \( ^3H \). \( ^1H \) and \( ^2H \) are stable, while \( ^3H \) has a half-life of 12.32 years.
Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosecond ( \(10^{-21} \ s \) ).
The symbols \( ^D \) and \( \mathbf{T} \) are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols \( ^2H \) and \( \mathbf{H} \), to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
