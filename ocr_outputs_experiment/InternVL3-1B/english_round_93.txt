Isotopes of hydrogen

Isotopes: \(^1H\), \(^2H\), and \(^3H\). \(^1H\) and \(^2H\) are stable, while \(^3H\) has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][(nb 1) Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1e7 zeptosecond (10^{-21} s).\] [5][6]

Hydrogen (He3) has three naturally occurring isotopes: \(^1H,\) \(^2H,\) and \(^3H.\) \(^1H\) and \(^2H\) are stable, while \(^3H\) has a half-life of 12.32 years.
