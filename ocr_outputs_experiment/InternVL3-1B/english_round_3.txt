Isotopes of hydrogen

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Hydrogen ( H,) has three naturally occurring isotopes: \(^{1}\mathrm{H},\) and \(^{3}\mathrm{H}\). \(^{1}\mathrm{H}\) and \(^{2}\mathrm{H}\) are stable, while \(^{3}\mathrm{H}\) has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb 1] Hydroisitopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosecond (10\(^{-21}\) s). [5][6]

Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeztosecond (10\(^{-21}\) s).
