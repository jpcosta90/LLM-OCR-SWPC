Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen (¹H), and ³H. ¹H and ²H are naturally occurring isotopes; all hydrogen isotopes also exist; all ³H have a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb 1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all ³H have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosec (10⁻²s). [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ²H is deuterium [7] and ³H is tritium. [8] The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deutium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas.
