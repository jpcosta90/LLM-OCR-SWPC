Isotopes of hydrogen

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Hydrogen (¹H), has three naturally occurring isotopes: ¹⁺, ²⁺, and ³⁺. ¹⁺ and ²⁺ are stable, while ³⁺ has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4]Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1ₜzeptosond (10⁻²¹ s). [5] Heavier radioisotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.
