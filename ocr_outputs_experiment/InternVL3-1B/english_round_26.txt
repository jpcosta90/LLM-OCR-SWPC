Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ²H is deuteri um and ³H is tritium. IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
