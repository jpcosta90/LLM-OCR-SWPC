Isotopes of hydrogen
**Article**
<sep>Talk

Hydrogen (¹H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ¹H, and ³H. ¹H and ²H are stable, while ³H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb 1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosecond (10⁻²¹ s). The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuteri and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols (²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulae).[9] With no neutrons, may be called protium to disambiguate.[10] During the early study of radioactive, some other heavy radioisotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.
