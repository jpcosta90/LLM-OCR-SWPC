Hydro, has three naturally occurring isotopes: 1H, and 13H. 1H and 2H are stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years.[4][nb]\[1] Hydro is highly unstable as compared to hydrogen-2 and is stable.[26] The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols 2H and 3H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
