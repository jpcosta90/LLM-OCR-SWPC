Isotopes of hydrogen

Isotopes: \( ^1H, ^2H, \) and \( ^3H \). \( ^1H \) and \( ^2H \) are stable, while \( ^3H \) has a half-life of 12.32 years. \( ^1H \) has a half-life of less than 1 zettasecond (10\(^{-21}\) s).  
Heavier isotopes also exist; all

Heavier isotopes remain in common use today: \( ^2H \) is deuteron and \( ^3H \) is tritium. The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deutrium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols \( ^2H \) and \( ^3H \), to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
