Isotopes of hydrogen
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes:
1H, 2H, and 3H. 1H and 2H are stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb 1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosecond (10−²¹ s). . [5][6]

Heavier isotopes, such as deuterium and tritium, are also allowed. The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuteron and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols 2H and 3H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
