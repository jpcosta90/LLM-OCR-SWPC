Isotopes of hydrogen

~: Talk

Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: 1H, and 3H. 1H and 2H are stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nB] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosec (10⁻²¹ s). [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: 1H is deuteron (7) and 3H is tritium. 1D and 3D are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols 1H and 3H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
