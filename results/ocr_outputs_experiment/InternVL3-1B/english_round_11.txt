Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ^1H, and ^3H. ^1H and ^2H are stable, while ^3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb 1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zetapend (10⁻²1 s). [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ^2H is deuteron [7] and ^3H is tritium.
