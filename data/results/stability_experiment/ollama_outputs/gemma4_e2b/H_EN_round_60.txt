Isotopes of hydrogen
Article Talk
Isotopes of hydrogen 1H, 2H, and 3H are
stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years.[4]nb 1
Heavier isotopes also exist; all
are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 102 seconds.[5][6]
Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in
common use today: 2H is deuterium[7] and 3H is tritium.[8] The symbols D and T are
sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and
Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic
symbols 2H and 3H, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas.[9]
Hydrogen has three naturally occurring isotopes: 1H, 2H, and 3H.
