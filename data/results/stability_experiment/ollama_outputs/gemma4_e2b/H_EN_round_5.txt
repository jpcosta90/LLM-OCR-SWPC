Isotopes of hydrogen
Article halkipedia
Isotopes of hydrogen $^{1}$H, $^{2}$H, and $^{3}$H are stable, while $^{3}$H has a half-life of 12.32 years.[4]nb 1
Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of 12.32 years.[5][6]
Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in common use today: $^{2}$H is deuterium[7] and $^{3}$H is tritium.[8] The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols $^{2}$H and $^{3}$H.
