Isotopes of hydrogen
Article halkipedia isotopes of hydrogen
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Hydrogen (¹H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ¹H, ²H, and ³H. The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas.[9] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are stable, while ³H has a half-life of 12.32 years.[4] During the early study of radioactivity, some other heavy radioisotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.
