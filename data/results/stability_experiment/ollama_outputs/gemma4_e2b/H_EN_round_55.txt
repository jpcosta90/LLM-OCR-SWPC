Isotopes of hydrogen
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Hydrogen (¹H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ¹H, ²H, and ³H. ¹H and ²H are
sometimes used for deuterium and tritium.[4]¹H and ²H are
stable, while ³H has a half-life of 12.32 years.[5][6]
Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in
common use today: ²H is deuterium[7] and ³H is tritium.[8] The symbols D and T are
sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC
isotopes said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic
symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of
chemical formulas.[9]
