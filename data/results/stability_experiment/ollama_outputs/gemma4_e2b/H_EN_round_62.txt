Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in common use today: $\text{}^{2}\text{H}$ is deuterium[7] and $\text{}^{3}\text{H}$ is tritium[8]. The symbols D and T are
sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols $\text{}^{2}\text{H}$ and $\text{}^{3}\text{H}$ to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas.[9]
