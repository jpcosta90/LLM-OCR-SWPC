From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Hydrogen ($\text{}^{1}\text{H}$) has three naturally occurring isotopes: $\text{}^{1}\text{H}$, $\text{}^{2}\text{H}$ and $\text{}^{3}\text{H}$
sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC ($\text{International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry}$) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols $\text{}^{2}\text{H}$ and $\text{}^{3}\text{H}$
to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas. [9]
Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in
common use today.
$\text{}^{2}\text{H}$ has a half-life of 12.32 years. [5]
