Isotopes of hydrogen $\text{¹H}$, $\text{²H}$, and $\text{³H}$ are stable, while $\text{³H}$ has a half-life of $12.32$ years.[4] $\text{nb}$
Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of $10^{-21}$ s.[5][6]
Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: $\text{²H}$ is deuterium[7] and $\text{³H}$ is tritium.[8] The symbols $\text{D}$ and $\text{T}$ are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC
