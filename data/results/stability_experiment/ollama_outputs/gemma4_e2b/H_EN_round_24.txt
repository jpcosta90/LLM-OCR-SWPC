Isotopes of hydrogen
Article Talk isotopes of hydrogen element half-life isotopes heavier isotopes and tritium The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols $^2\text{H}$ and $^3\text{H}$, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas. Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in common use today: $^2\text{H}$ has a half-life of $12.32$ years.[4] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of $10^{-21}$ s.[5]
