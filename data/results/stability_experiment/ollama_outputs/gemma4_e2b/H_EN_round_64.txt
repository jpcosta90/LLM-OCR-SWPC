Isotopes of hydrogen
Article Talk isotopes of hydrogen
$\text{H}_2\text{H}$, $\text{H}$, and $\text{H}$ are stable, while $\text{}^3\text{H}$ has a half-life of $12.32$ years. $[\text{4}]\text{nb} [10]$ Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than $10^{21}$ seconds $[\text{5}][6]$
Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in common use today: $\text{}^1\text{H}$, with no neutrons, may be called protium to disambiguate. $[\text{10}]$ During the early study of radioactivity, some other heavy radioisotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.
