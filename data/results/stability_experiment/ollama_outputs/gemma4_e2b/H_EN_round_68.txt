Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in common use today: $\text{2}^1\text{H}$ has half-life of $12.32$ years. $\text{1}^2\text{H}$ and $\text{3}^2\text{H}$ are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC ($\text{International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry}$) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols $\text{2}^1\text{H}$ and $\text{3}^2\text{H}$ are
$\text{1}^1\text{H}$, with no neutrons, may be called protium to disambiguate. During the early study of radioactivity, some other heavy radioisotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.
