Hydrogen ($\text{}^1\text{H}$) has three naturally occurring isotopes: $\text{}^1\text{H}$, $\text{}^2\text{H}$ and $\text{}^3\text{H}$. $\text{}^2\text{H}$ are sometimes used for deuterium and $\text{}^3\text{H}$ is tritium.[8] The symbols $\text{D}$ and $\text{T}$ are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols $\text{}^1\text{H}$, $\text{}^2\text{H}$ and $\text{}^3\text{H}$, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas.[9]
