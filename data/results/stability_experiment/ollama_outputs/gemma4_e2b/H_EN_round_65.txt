Isotopes of hydrogen
Article Talk isotopes of hydrogen
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Hydrogen ($\text{}^1\text{H}$), the free isotopes have different names in common use today: $\text{}^2\text{H}$ is deuterium[7] and $\text{}^3\text{H}$ is tritium[8]. The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols $\text{}^1\text{H}$, with no neutrons, may be called protium to disambiguate.[10] During the early study of radioactivity, some other heavy radioisotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.
