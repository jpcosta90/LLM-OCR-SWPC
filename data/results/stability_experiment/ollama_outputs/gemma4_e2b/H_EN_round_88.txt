Isotopes of hydrogen
Article Talk
Isotopes of hydrogen $\text{}^{1}\text{H}$, $\text{}^{2}\text{H}$, and $\text{}^{3}\text{H}$ are stable, while $\text{}^{2}\text{H}$ has half-life of 12.32 years.[4]nb 1Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of 12.32 years.[5]
Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names in common use today: $\text{}^{1}\text{H}$, with no neutrons, may be called protium to disambiguate.[10] During the early study of radioactivity, some other heavy radioisotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.
