Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of
testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe.[1]:49–71[2] Modern science is
typically divided into – or three branches:[3] Natural philosophy of classical antiquity and
medieval scholarship, whereby formal attempts were made to provide explanations
of events in the physical world as natural causes; while further advances,
including the introduction of the Hindu-Arabic numeral system, were made during the
Golden Age of India and Islamic Golden Age.[13]:12[14]:1–26[15][16]:13[1]:63–192
