The term "science" is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses or theories about the universe.[14][79–82] Modern science[36], particularly physics , chemistry [ clarification needed ](i) includes natural philosophy (ii), which aims to study the physical world, and the social sciences,[ 50 ][ note 1 ]which study individuals and societies. While referred to as the formal sciences ,[iii][47]the study of logic mathematics,and theoretical computer science are typically regarded as separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method[as main methodology].[68]: [i](iv)Meanwhile, applied sciences apply knowledge.[35]
