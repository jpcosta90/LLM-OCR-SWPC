|  | Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses about[ clarification needed ]the universe. Modern scientific inquiry typically involves two or three major branches: theory, experimentally verified theories; empirical evidence based on observation [2] ; mathematical models used to describe natural phenomena.[3][4]

Scientific study is often divided into six main disciplines that are commonly referred to as the formal sciences : mathematics , logic and computer science (which rely primarily upon deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method ) whereas they use deduction . While physics refers mainly on empirical observation, applied sciences such as engineering [5] focus more directly towards practical applications. These branches typically overlap with each other in terms of methodology.[6][7]
