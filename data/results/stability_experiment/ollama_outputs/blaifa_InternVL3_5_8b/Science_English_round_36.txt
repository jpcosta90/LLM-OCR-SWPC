Scientific method and scientific study are terms that refer to ways or methods which can be used in order to obtain knowledge. In general use these words denote any system for obtaining new information about anything — particularly with regard to nature.[1] The word is also sometimes[2][3]
used as a synonym of technology . However , most scientists would say the two are distinct.

Study hypothesis and prediction - The science aims to identify general truths or laws in nature.[5] In modern times , it is typically introduced by two—or three—major branches:93 the natural sciences ( biology , chemistry, physics) which study matter; mathematical logic ;[10][74]
social studies like psychology  which try and explain how individuals think,[82] learn to reason.[6]

The term "natural science" is used in contrast with social science . The latter includes the formal sciences : mathematics , computer
science, theoretical physics (especially statistical mechanics)[95].[10][74]
For example they may rely on deductive reasoning instead of experimental methods as their main methodology. For instance mathematical logic relies upon abstract notions and proofs rather than observation[6]

Modern science is commonly understood to be a pursuit which builds up knowledge about the natural world.[8] It contrasts with other disciplines such as history , literature or philosophy . Natural sciences are typically regarded separately because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of experimental methods. For example mathematics does not involve direct testing in nature but rather relies upon abstract notions and proofs.

Some systems[97][106]
are still used by scientists to describe their activities.[85] The study is based around the scientific method which involves making observations , forming hypotheses about them, predicting outcomes from those initial assumptions or premises then either confirming these through experiments [ 3 ] Or disproving via falsification .

The history of science spans most historical records with earliest identifiable predecessors dating back to ancient civilizations such as Egypt during its Bronze Age (circa c . 2504–1768 BCE) and Mesopotamia circa （c. 3,900–1, 2th century BCE). Their contributions include mathematics astronomy astrology medicine[ i ] agriculture , metallurgy etc.[ j ]

Later advances came about during the Hellenistic age where philosophers such as Aristotle developed formal systems of thought that would later influence medieval scholarship whereby attempts were made to provide explanations for events in physical world on natural causes ; while further improvements occurred with Newtonian mechanics and other developments within mathematics physics chemistry biology medicine botany astronomy astrology geology geography etc.[ k ]
