The word "science" comes from Latin: scientia ("knowledge").[a] The term was first used in Europe during Renaissance by Francis Bacon [b][c]

Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses . Predictions about the universe , as measured by experiments or observation, are made using scientific models. Modern science can be divided into three major branches: 1) natural sciences (physics,[2] chemistry,and biology), which study nature;[3][4]

The term "science" was used in a wide sense to describe any systematized knowledge.[5](p78)[6a–d],[e] including philosophy . Philosophers refer to the formal sciences, namely mathematics , theoretical physics and logic as being separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of scientific method as their main methodology. Meanwhile applied science are disciplines that use existing theories or principles in order for practical applications.[2][f]

The history of Science spans the majority of the historical record with earliest identifiable predecessors to modern science dating back from Bronze Age and Mesopotamia (3000–1750 BCE), Ancient Egypt , Greek contributions, astronomy . The scientific revolution marks a point where natural philosophy started being separated into physical world based on empirical research rather than relying solely upon religious doctrine as was the case during Golden age of India[ g ] Islamic golden Age and Hindu-Arabic culture in middle east.[h]
