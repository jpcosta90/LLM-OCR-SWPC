Science  Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe.[14][8–7] Modern science[26][30] typically divides into two or three—major branches:9,10 The natural sciences,[5] which study the physical world; and the social sciences,[2],  which study individuals and societies. While commonly referred to as formal sciences , the study of logic , mathematics, statistics,and philosophy[78][a ] are typically regarded as separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method as their main methodology.[619] Meanwhile, applied engineering is disciplines that build upon knowledge acquired by scientists and medicines.
