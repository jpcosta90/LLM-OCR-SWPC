Hydrogen (H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: H-1 , D and T . The symbols for these atoms are sometimes confused with atomic numbers; however, they do not represent number of protons. Hydrogen always consists only one proton but varies in its neutron count to give different forms known as isotope s.
