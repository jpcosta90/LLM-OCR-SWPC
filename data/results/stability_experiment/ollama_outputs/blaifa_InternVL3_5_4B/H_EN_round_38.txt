Isotopes of hydrogen

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia


Hydrogen (H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: 1 H , 2 H and 3 H . The most abundant isotope is protium or simply “hydro- gen,” which contains no neutrons; it may be called deuterium to disambiguate.[a] During the early study of radioactivity, some other heavy radioactive isotopes were given names, but such names are rarely used today.

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: 1 H =deut er ium( D ) and 3 H=triti um.( T ).
