Isotopes of hydrogen

An isotopic variant (or simply an "isotope") is a type 
of atomic nucleus that has one particular number of protons and neutrons, in contrast to other possible numbers for each element.
The terms nuclide or nuclear isomer may also be used.

(H-2), protium (H-1) which have the same chemical properties but different physical ones. The most abundant form of hydrogen, with no neutrons,
is known as "protion" to distinguish it from other heavy ions.

The chemical symbol for an element's isotopes is the letter "H" with a superscript to denote mass number or atomic weight,
while subscripts show proton numbers. The term most often used in chemistry refers only 
to those where both are identical; otherwise, they're called mixed.
In these cases it can be confusing because this name also applies
to compounds of hydrogen and another element.
