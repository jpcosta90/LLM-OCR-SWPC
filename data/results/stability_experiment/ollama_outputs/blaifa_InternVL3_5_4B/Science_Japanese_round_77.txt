 science, mathematics is related to the world.  Scientific research uses experimental techniques and empirical evidence.
 Mathematics has a theoretical component that allows scientists to make predictions about future events based on current observations.

 scientific inquiry often begins with making an observation or asking a question. This can be done through direct experience by using our senses, though it may not always lead us directly to the answer we seek  [14] . Scientists then form hypotheses and test them against new evidence.
 Science uses systematic methods for gathering information about observations of natural phenomena: hypothesis formation, experimentation (the testing phase) or observing patterns in nature. These phases are typically repeated until all aspects have been thoroughly investigated.

 scientific theories provide a framework within which the physical universe can be understood  [15] . Scientific research is often organized into different fields to focus on specific areas and facilitate communication among researchers with similar interests.
 Theories should also withstand rigorous testing over time; otherwise, they may need modification or replacement. In addition, peer review (a process by other scientists evaluating the validity of published work) ensures that scientific findings are reliable.

 Scientific knowledge is typically communicated through publication in academic journals which undergo a thorough evaluation before being accepted for inclusion within their respective fields.
 This helps maintain high standards and prevents dissemination of false information into public discourse. Moreover it allows future researchers access to previously unexplored ideas or discoveries made during the course years past without having had an opportunity themselves make those connections firsthand.

 However not all advancements in understanding our world fit neatly into existing frameworks; some may challenge prevailing notions entirely, prompting re-evaluation and potential paradigm shifts.
 Ultimately science aims at explaining how things work rather than simply describing what exists. This requires a combination of empirical data collection with logical reasoning about patterns observed over time leading to comprehensive theories capable enough withstand scrutiny from multiple perspectives across various domains.

 Mathematics plays an essential role in this process by providing tools for modeling complex systems accurately and predicting outcomes under different conditions while minimizing uncertainty inherent due limitations available technology at any given moment.
