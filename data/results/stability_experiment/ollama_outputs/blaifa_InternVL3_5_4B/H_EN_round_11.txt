Isotopes of hydrogen

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia


Hydrogen (H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: H1 , H2 and 3H . The stable ones are called protium to disambiguate [4] during early chemistry work with radioactive materials or very small quantities. Other heavy isotope which exist only in nature for short time, the others have a half-life of less than one year (or even shorter). These isotopes usually occur naturally by decay from other heavier elements and are produced synthetically.
