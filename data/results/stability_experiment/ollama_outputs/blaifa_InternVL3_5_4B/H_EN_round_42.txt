Hydrogen (H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: 1 H ,2 H and34 H . The most stable isotope with an atomic number of one; deuterium was identified in early studies. Hydroxyl radicals are unstable but can be produced by lasers.

The symbol for hydrogen-1 atom, which contains no neutrons (in other words the nucleus consists only of a single proton), may also simply denote this element or its ions and is often used interchangeably with the chemical symbols H+ ,H– . The latter two have been called "proton" to disambiguate.[a] During early studies, some heavy isotopes were given names. For example:

Hydrogen was first named for its properties as a radioactive element by the discovery that it emits radiation. Some other heavy radioisotopes were given names, but such terms are rarely used today.
