水素 is the only element for which no stable isotopes exist. It has long been known that several radioactive decay modes occur in natural samples of water and hydrogen compounds. The various names have not always stayed consistent: until 1950 all naturally occurring nuclei were called tritium (³H). Atoms with mass numbers other than three are now referred to as heavier elements by the International Union for Pure And Applied Chemistry, but they can still be found in large quantities when produced artificially.
