**Science is **a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses [1] *and predictions* about the universe. The natural sciences are concerned with non-living systems (e.g., physics), while social scientists study individuals or groups within living societies, typically referred to as "the formal" sciences; however science is a broad field that includes both areas and all other types of knowledge not directly related in practice but can be useful for understanding the general scientific method. [1] Science has traditionally been regarded as separate from mathematics because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of *deductive* rather than "the Scientific Method" 89[6][7]*as their main methodology.[2]

The history **of science** spans most major periods, with earliest identifiable predecessors to modern science dating back the Bronze Age in Egypt and Mesopotamia (300BC-150 BC).
