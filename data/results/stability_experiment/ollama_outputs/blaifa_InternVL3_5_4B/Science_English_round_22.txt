Science  Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe.[14][17–2] Modern science[35] typically refers to what was formerly known as natural sciences, which study the physical world,[8069a] or more generally all formal disciplines. The social sciences are those branches that deal with human beings; these include psychology (which is often considered a sub-field of philosophy), anthropology and sociology.[14][27–35]
