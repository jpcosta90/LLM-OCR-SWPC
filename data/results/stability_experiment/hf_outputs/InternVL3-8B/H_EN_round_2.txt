Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen (\(^{1}\!\mathrm{H}\)) has three naturally occurring isotopes: \({\displaystyle ^{1}{\mathrm{~H~}}}\), \({\displaystyle ^{2}{\mathrm{~H~}}}\), and \({\displaystyle ^{3}{\mathrm{~H}}}\). \({\displaystyle ^{1}{\mathrm{~H~}}}\) and \({\displaystyle ^{2}{\mathrm{~H~}}}\) are stable, while \({\displaystyle ^{3}{\mathrm{~H}}}\) has a \( \text{half-life of } 12.32 \text{ years}. \!\![4][\text{nb }1] \) Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than \( 1 \text{ zepto-} \text{second}\) \( (10^{-21} \text{ s}). \!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\! [5][6] \)
