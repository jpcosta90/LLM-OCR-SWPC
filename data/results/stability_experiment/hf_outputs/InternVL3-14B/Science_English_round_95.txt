Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of  
testable ["hypotheses"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypothesis) and ["predictions"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prediction) about the universe.["1"]^49–71["2"] 
Modern science is  
typically divided into two – or three – major branches:[3] ["the natural sciences"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_science), which  
study the ["physical world"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_science), and the ["social sciences"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_science),  
which study individuals and societies.[4][5] While referred to as the ["formal sciences"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formal_science), the  
study of ["logic"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic), ["mathematics"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics), and ["theoretical computer science"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theoretical_computer_science) are typically regarded as separate because they rely  
on ["deductive reasoning"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deductive_reasoning) instead of the ["scientific method"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_method) as their main  
methodology.[6][7][8][9] Meanwhile, ["applied sciences"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_science) are disciplines that use scientific  
knowledge for practical purposes, such as engineering and medicine.[10][11][12]

["The history of science"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_science) spans the majority of the historical record, with the earliest  
identifiable predecessors to modern science dating to the ["Bronze Age"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age) in ["Egypt"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt) and  
["Mesopotamia"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesopotamia) (c.3000–1200 BCE). Their contributions to ["mathematics"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics),  
["astronomy"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy), and medicine entered and shaped the Greek ["natural philosophy"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_philosophy) of ["classical antiquity"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_ancient) and  
later ["medieval scholarship"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval), whereby formal attempts were made to provide explanations  
of events in the ["physical world"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_world) based on natural causes; while further advances,  
including the introduction of the ["Hindu–Arabic numeral system"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindu–Arabic_numeral_system), were made  
during the ["Golden Age of India"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_India) and ["Islamic Golden Age"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Age_of_Islamic_civilization).["13"1:12][4]1:26["15"1][6"1][13":163–192
