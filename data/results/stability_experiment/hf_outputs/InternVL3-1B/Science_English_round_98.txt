Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organizes knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe. Modern sciences are typically divided into two – or – three – major branches. The physical world and the social sciences, which study the physical world, and the social sciences, which study individuals and the social sciences, be they separated because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method as their main methodology. Meanwhile, applied sciences are disciplines that use scientific knowledge for practical purposes, such as engineering and medicine. Modern science is likely to rely on deductive reasoning rather than the scientific method because they study individuals and use them as separate apart from each other.
