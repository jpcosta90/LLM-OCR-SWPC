Hydrogen (¹H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: \(^{1}\!H\) , \(^{2}\!\mathrm{H} ,\) and \(^{3}\!\mathrm{H}\). \(^{1}\!\mathrm{H}\) and \(^{2}\!\mathrm{H} \) are stable, while \(^{3}\!\mathrm{H}\) has a half-life of 12.32 years. [5][nb1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 ztosec (10\(^{-21}\) s).
