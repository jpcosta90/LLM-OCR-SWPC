**Isotopes of hydrogen**

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ^1H, ^2H, and ^3H. 1H and 2H are stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4[9] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zebtose (10⁻²¹ s). [5[6]]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have differing names that remain in common use today: ^2H is deuterium and ^3H is tritium. The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ^2H and ^3H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
