There are three naturally occurring isotopes of hydrogen: ^{1}H, ^{2}H, and ^{3}H. ^{1}H and ^{2}H are stable, while ^{3}H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb]1[/it] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zebosillion (10−21 s). [5][br][br]hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ^{2}H is deuterium and ^{3}H is tritium.
