Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: 1H, 2H, and 3H. 1H and 2H are stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb1] Helier isotope  1H and 2H are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptosecond (10⁻²¹ s). [5][6] The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols 2H and 3H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
