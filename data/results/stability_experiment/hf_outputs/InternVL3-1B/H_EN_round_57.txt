Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ²H, ³H. ¹H and ²H are stable, while ³H has a half-Life of 12.32 years. [4][nb1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-Life of less than 1 zeptosec (10⁻²¹ s). [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: deuterium and tritium; IUPAC accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetic sorting of chemical formulas.
