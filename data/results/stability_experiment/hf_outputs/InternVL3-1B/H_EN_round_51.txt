Hydrogen (~hydrogen) has three naturally occuring isotopes: 1H, 2H, and 3H. 1H and 2H are stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years.
[2H] is deuterium [7] and [3H] is tritium. The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuteron and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols \(^2\mathbf{H}\) and \(^3\mathbf{H}\), to avoid confusion in alphabetic ordering of chemical formulas.
