1H, 2H, and 3H. 1H and 2H are stable, while 3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [5][6] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zep-topond (10−21 s). [5][6]

Hydrogen (1H) is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today. 2H is deuterium and 3H is tritium. The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols 2H and 3H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
