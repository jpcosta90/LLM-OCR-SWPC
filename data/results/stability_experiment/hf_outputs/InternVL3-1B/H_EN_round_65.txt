Isotopes of hydrogen
Article Talk

Hydrogen (₁H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ¹H, ²H, and ³H. ¹H and ²H are stable, while ³H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb=1] Heavier isotopes also exist; all are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zep-tosec (10⁻²¹ s). [5][nb=6] Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ²H is deuterium and ³H is tritium. The symbols D and T are sometimes used forDeuterium and tritium; DUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in al-phabetic sorting of chemical formulas.
