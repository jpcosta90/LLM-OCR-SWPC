Hydrogen (1H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ¹^1H, ²^H, and ³^H. ¹^1H and ²^H are stable, while ³^H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4][nb 1] Hear is also synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zep-topic (10^{-21} s). [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ²^H is deuterium and ³^H is tritium. । The symbols Д and ³^H are sometimes used for deuterdium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas [9] with no neutrons, may be called protium to disambiguate.
