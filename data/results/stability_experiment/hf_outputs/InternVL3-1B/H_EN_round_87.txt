Isotopes of hydrogen

Hydrogen (¹H) has three naturally occurring isotopes: ^1H, ^2H, and ^3H. ^1H and ^2H are stable, while ^3H has a half-life of 12.32 years. ^3H has a half-life of less than 1 zetsecond (10−²s). [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ^2H is deuterium (^7) and ^3H is tritium (^8). The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deuterium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alfabetec sorting of chemical formulas.
