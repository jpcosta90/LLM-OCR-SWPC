Isotopes of hydrogen

## Talk

Hydrogen (¹H) has three naturally arising isotopes: ¹H, ²H, and ³H. ¹H and ²H are stable, while ³H has a half-life of 12.32 years. [4] Heavier isotopes also exist; all  are synthetic and have a half-life of less than 1 zeptoSEC [5][6]

Hydrogen is the only element whose isotopes have different names that remain in common use today: ²H is deuterium and ³H is tritium.  The symbols D and T are sometimes used for deutium and tritium; IUPAC (International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry) accepts said symbols, but recommends the standard isotopic symbols ²H and ³H, to avoid confusion in alphabetical sorting of chemical formulas.
