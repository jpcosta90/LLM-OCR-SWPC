Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe. Modern science is typically divided into two or three – or three – major branches: the natural sciences, which study the physical world, and the social sciences, which study individuals and societies.[1][49–71][2] These sciences are typically regarded as separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method. Meanwhile, applied sciences are disciplines that use scientific knowledge for practical purposes, such as engineering and medicine.
