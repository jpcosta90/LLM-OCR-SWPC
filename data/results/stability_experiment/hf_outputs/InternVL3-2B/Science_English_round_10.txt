Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe. Modern science is typically divided into two or three – or three – major branches: the natural sciences, which study the physical world, and the social sciences, which study individuals and societies. Meanwhile, computer science is a discipline that uses scientific methodology as its main approach. Computer science, apart from focusing on the design of algorithm and software engineering, might also entail applied sciences disciplines that use scientific knowledge for practical purposes, such as engineering and medicine. The history of science spans the majority of the historic record, with the earliest identifiable predecessors to modern science dating to the Bronze Age in Egypt and Mesopotamia (c. 3300–1000 BCE). Their contributions to mathematics, astronomy, and medicine entered and shaped the Greek natural philosophy of classical antiquity and later medieval scholarship, whereby formal attempts were made to provide explanations of events in the physical world based on natural causes; further advances, including the introduction of the Hindi–Arabic numeral system, were made during the Golden Age of India and Islamic Golden Age.
