Modern science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe. Modern sciences are typically divided into two – or three – major branches: the natural sciences, which study the physical world, and the social sciences, which study individuals and societies.[3] While referred to as the formal sciences, the study of logic, mathematics, computer science are typically regarded as separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method as their main methodology.
