Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe. Modern sciences are typically divided into two or three – or three – major branches. The natural sciences, which study the physical world, and the social sciences, which study individuals and societies. Modern sciences are typically regarded as separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method as their main methodology. Meanwhile, applied sciences are disciplines that use scientific knowledge for practical purposes, such as engineering and medicine. Formal sciences are typically regarded as separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method as their main methodology. In everyday use, applied sciences are commonly referred to as the "formal sciences," as they are typically regarded as separate from natural sciences because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method.

The history of science spans the majority of the historical record, with the earliest identifiable predecessors to modern science dating to the Bronze Age in Egypt and Mesopotamia (~ 3,000–1,200 BCE). Their contributions to mathematics, astronomy, and medicine entered and shaped the Greek natural philosophy of classical antiquity and later medieval scholarship, whereby formal attempts were made to provide explanations of events in the physical world based on natural causes; while further advances, including the introduction of the **Hindu-Arabic numeral system**, were made during the **Golden Age of India** and **Islamic Golden Age**. The natural sciences, which typically rely on observations and experiments to develop theories, are typical of science known as the formal sciences, the study of formal systems and their properties, including control theory; formal methods, which are also known as the mathematical sciences, that use formal methods to model and solve problems, such as the mathematical treatment of phenomena; and logical and formal computer science that includes the formal specification of systems and the formal verification of certain properties about them.
