Modern science is typically regarded as separate because they rely on deductive reasoning instead of the scientific method as their main methodology.[6][7][8][9] Meanwhile, applied sciences are disciplines that use scientific knowledge for practical purposes, such as engineering and medicine.[10][11][12] The history of science spans the majority of the historical record, with the earliest identifiable predecessors to modern science dating to the **Bronze Age** in Egypt and Mesopotamia (c. 3000–1200 B.C.E).
